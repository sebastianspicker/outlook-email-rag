# BA5 Language And Rhetoric Signals

Version: `1`

This BA5 layer is limited to per-message rhetorical cues. It is not a behavioural finding engine and it does not make legal conclusions.

## Signals

### `dismissiveness`

- Meaning:
  - minimizing or shorthand-dismissive phrasing that pushes the recipient's concern aside
- Example cues:
  - `as already stated`
  - `please just`
  - `simply`
  - `wie schon mehrfach mitgeteilt`
  - `nochmals zur Klarstellung`

### `ridicule`

- Meaning:
  - openly derisive wording rather than neutral criticism
- Example cues:
  - `ridiculous`
  - `absurd`
  - `laughable`
  - `nonsense`

### `patronizing_wording`

- Meaning:
  - wording that positions the recipient as less informed or less capable
- Example cues:
  - `let me explain again`
  - `as you surely know`
  - `for your understanding`
  - `perhaps you are unaware`
  - `zu Ihrer Orientierung`
  - `ich führe das gern noch einmal aus`

### `implicit_accusation`

- Meaning:
  - framing the recipient as having failed, refused, or omitted something without neutral phrasing
- Example cues:
  - `you failed`
  - `you refused`
  - `your omission`

### `competence_framing`

- Meaning:
  - wording that frames the recipient as confused, unreliable, or otherwise less credible
- Example cues:
  - `confused`
  - `misunderstood`
  - `unreliable`
  - `not capable`
  - `Ihre Darstellung ist unzutreffend`
  - `das scheint Sie zu überfordern`

### `institutional_pressure_framing`

- Meaning:
  - process, escalation, HR, or compliance language used as pressure rather than neutral coordination
- Example cues:
  - `for the record`
  - `failure to comply`
  - `escalate`
  - `HR`
  - `formal process`

### `strategic_ambiguity`

- Meaning:
  - vague concern-framing without clearly stating the underlying allegation or basis
- Example cues:
  - `concerns have been raised`
  - `it appears`
  - `it seems`
  - `questions remain`
  - `nach hiesigem Kenntnisstand`
  - `der Eindruck entsteht`

### `selective_politeness`

- Meaning:
  - formal courtesy markers used together with distancing or corrective framing that can read as selectively cold
- Example cues:
  - `with all due respect`
  - `kindly note`
  - `wir bitten Sie höflich um Kenntnisnahme`

### `procedural_intimidation`

- Meaning:
  - documentation, escalation, or rule-based language that can function as pressure rather than neutral coordination
- Example cues:
  - `this will be documented`
  - `wir sehen uns gehalten, dies festzuhalten`
  - `eine weitere Eskalation behalten wir uns vor`

### `passive_aggressive_deflection`

- Meaning:
  - apparently neutral closure language that redirects responsibility or shuts down further discussion
- Example cues:
  - `I trust this clarifies matters`
  - `weitere Rückfragen erübrigen sich`
  - `ich gehe davon aus, dass damit alles geklärt ist`

### `status_marking`

- Meaning:
  - hierarchy or institutional status is foregrounded in a way that can add pressure beyond the substance
- Example cues:
  - `as your manager`
  - `als Leitung`
  - `als zuständige Stelle`

### `gaslighting_like_contradiction`

- Meaning:
  - low-confidence contradiction-style pattern that combines prior-statement framing with recipient-confusion framing
- Example cues:
  - `as already explained`
  - `you are mistaken`
  - `you misunderstood`
  - `you are confused`
  - `wie schon mehrfach mitgeteilt`
  - `Ihre Darstellung ist unzutreffend`

## Boundaries

- These are candidate rhetorical signals only.
- BA5 keeps authored text and quoted text separate.
- Quoted-block analysis stays subordinate to quote-attribution uncertainty.
- A rhetorical signal does not by itself prove hostility, retaliation, discrimination, or mobbing.
- German formal office language is not automatically hostile; cues should be read conservatively and often need behavior or recurrence support.
- Later BA phases must add behavioural tagging, recurrence, counter-indicators, and evidence-strength scoring before stronger conclusions are made.
